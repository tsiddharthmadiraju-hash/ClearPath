# ClearPath — Demo Pitch Script

**Total runtime: ~3 minutes.** Structure leads with *impact* (what changes for the
user), then shows the tool working. Live-demo cues are in `[SCREEN: …]` blocks —
read the narration aloud while doing the on-screen action.

**Before you record, have ready:**
- A phone (or screen recording) open to **https://clearpath-dtrg.onrender.com** — hard-refresh once so it's warm.
- A printed/on-screen **eviction "Notice to Vacate"** ($1,200 owed, 5 days to respond) to photograph, OR the **Eviction Notice** example chip as a reliable backup.
- Sound on. Practice the taps once so the timing is smooth.

---

## 0:00 – 0:30 — The problem & who it affects  *(lead with the human, not the tech)*

> "This is a notice to vacate. It says a family has **five days** to respond or they
> lose their home — but it's buried in legal language they've never been taught to
> read. **One in three U.S. adults** struggles to understand documents exactly like
> this: eviction notices, benefits letters, medical bills, school and utility
> warnings. The people hit hardest — immigrants, seniors, low-income families — are
> the ones with the **least** access to a lawyer who could explain it. The deadline
> isn't the scary part. **Not understanding it** is."

`[SCREEN: hold up the real letter to the camera. Let the judges see a wall of dense legal text for a beat.]`

---

## 0:30 – 1:30 — What ClearPath does & how  *(now show the tool)*

> "ClearPath turns that letter into a plan — in under fifteen seconds. Watch."

`[SCREEN: open the site. Tap "Take a Photo," frame the letter, tap the shutter, "Use this photo."]`

> "It reads the document with Claude, and the first thing it gives back is **plain
> language**: 'Your landlord says you owe $1,200 and you have 5 days to pay or move
> out.' No jargon."

`[SCREEN: the "What this means" card appears. Scroll to the checklist.]`

> "Then it does what a letter never does — it tells you **what to do**, in order,
> with **deadlines and urgency**. Red means act now. And if any step is still
> confusing, you tap **'Explain this step in more detail'** and ClearPath breaks
> that one step into simple sub-steps a nervous person can actually follow."

`[SCREEN: tap "▾ Explain this step in more detail" — the numbered mini-steps slide open.]`

> "Finally, it connects you to **real, free, local help** — with a phone number and
> even a script of exactly what to say when you call. And the whole thing works in
> **14 languages**, instantly."

`[SCREEN: scroll to "Who can help" → tap a call-script. Then tap the language switch → pick Spanish → the entire result re-translates in place.]`

---

## 1:30 – 2:00 — The one moment that matters  *(before → after)*

> "Here's the moment. **Before ClearPath:** a parent stares at this notice, doesn't
> realize the clock is five days not thirty, misses the window, and a missed
> deadline becomes an eviction on their record. **After ClearPath:** thirty seconds
> later they're looking at 'Call your landlord **today**' marked urgent, a checklist
> they can tick off, and a tap-to-call button to **211** for free legal aid. Same
> letter. Same person. The difference is they now know **what to do before the
> deadline** — and that they're not alone."

`[SCREEN: tap the red "211" phone number so the dialer pops up. Then tap "Talk to a real person."]`

---

## 2:00 – 2:30 — Responsible AI & the human role

> "We built this for people in vulnerable moments, so the responsible-AI choices are
> the product. **One:** ClearPath never pretends to be a lawyer — every result is
> labeled with a **confidence level**, and if a photo is blurry it **lowers its
> confidence instead of guessing**. **Two:** it never replaces a human — every
> single result ends with **'Talk to a real person'** and routes to free legal aid
> and 211. AI is the bridge to help, not the final word. **Three:** **privacy** —
> documents are never stored on a server; your history lives only on your device.
> Plus input sanitization, prompt-injection protection, and rate limiting under the
> hood."

`[SCREEN: point to the confidence pill at the top, then the "Your documents are never stored" line on the home screen.]`

---

## 2:30 – 3:00 — What we'd build next

> "Next, we go deeper and wider. **Location-aware resources** — enter your ZIP and
> get the legal aid office on *your* street, not a national hotline. **Read-aloud
> and voice input** for users who struggle with reading itself. **Partnerships**
> with libraries, clinics, and legal-aid nonprofits who already sit with these
> families. And an **offline mode** so a saved plan works even without data. The
> goal is simple: **nobody should lose their home, their benefits, or their health
> coverage just because a letter was too hard to read.** That's ClearPath."

`[SCREEN: end on the home screen with the tagline "Received a letter you don't understand? ClearPath reads it and tells you exactly what to do."]`

---

### Delivery tips
- **Lead with the letter, not the logo.** The first 10 seconds should make a judge feel the stress.
- **Keep the live demo to ONE document** end-to-end — depth beats breadth.
- If the network is slow (Render free-tier cold start), use the **Eviction Notice example chip** — it's instant and identical on screen.
- Say the numbers out loud: "$1,200," "5 days," "14 languages," "211." Concrete > abstract.
- Practiced runtime should land at **2:50–3:00**. Cut adjectives, not steps, if you run long.
